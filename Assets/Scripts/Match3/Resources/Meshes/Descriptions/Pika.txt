Today, the Pika (or rock rabbit) is typically found at higher elevations where there is sufficient snow in the winter to preserve the piles of hay they use to survive the winter. But in the Pleistocene we find Pika at much lower elevations - apparently when it was cold enough, they had more room to roam! Because Pika are so sensitive to temperatures, warming global temperatures are shrinking their potential habitat range. While Pika are not found in the Lake Chewaucan region today, we do see them in the fossil record - right up until around 12,000 years ago when the climate became too warm for them.

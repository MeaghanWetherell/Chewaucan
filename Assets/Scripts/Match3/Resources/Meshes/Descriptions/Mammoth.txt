We know that the people at the Paisley Caves at least occasionally would consume mammoth. Or mastodon. We aren't sure which, because our evidence comes not from bones but from protein residues! Scientists took samples from grinding stones and obsidian flakes and found elephant proteins on both... but weren't able to distinguish which species was present. These samples date to 13,500 years ago. However, it's more likely to be mammoth than mastodon. Mastodons tend to prefer thick forests to the open habitat found at Paisley Caves at the time, so its probably a grass-eating mammoth and not a tree-eating mastodon. Mammoths bones have also been found at Fossil Lake from around 650,000 years ago to 100,000 years ago.

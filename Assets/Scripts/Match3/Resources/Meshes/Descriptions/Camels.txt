There are two different types  of camels found in the fossil and archeological record near Lake Chewaucan. Camelops was a giant camel like the dromedary or the bactrian camel of today, and is found from around 14,600 to 13,600 years ago. Hemiauchenia is the ancestor of modern lamas and vicugnas (aka, wild alpacas), and is known from a single specimen that was about 14,000 years old. Both species are also found at Fossil Lake, though Camelops is more common. It seems that like the horses, Camelops came down for a drink and got permanently stuck in the mud. Camelops is found at Fossil Lake from 650,000 years ago through to nearly 16,000 years ago. Hemiauchenia was not as common, and is found from 650,000 years ago to around 47,000 years ago.

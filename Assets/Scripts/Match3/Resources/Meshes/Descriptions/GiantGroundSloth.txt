Giant ground sloths were giant, land-bound ancestors of the modern tree sloths. While modern sloths are limited to the rainforests of South and Central America, giant ground sloths ranged across both continents and are found in all sorts of environments. Sloths are known from a few of the archeological and paleontological sites in the Lake Chewaucan area, including hair from the Paisley Caves and skeletal remains from Fossil Lake. While it's hard to say exactly what species of sloth produced the hair, the bones belong to Paramylodon. Though not the biggest of the giant ground sloths, Paramylodon had strong forearms that may have been used to build tunnels and burrows.

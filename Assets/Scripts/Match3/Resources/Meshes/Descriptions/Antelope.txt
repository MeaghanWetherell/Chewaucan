Pronghorn antelope are more closely related to giraffes than to African antelopes. They are open habitat specialists and are found across eastern Oregon today. The bones and hair of a single individual are found in the Paisley caves and it looks like the antelope was butchered and eaten by people who lived there around 12,000 years ago. The paleontology site at Fossil Lake also seems to preserve a single pronghorn, though this one is from around 30,000 years ago. When this area was colder and wetter, pronghorn antelope were simply not as common as they are today.

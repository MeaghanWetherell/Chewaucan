There are multiple species of kangaroo rats found in the fossil record at the Paisley Caves. The Ord's Kangaroo rat can live in a variety of shrublands and is found in the Pleistocene. The Chisel-toothed Kangaroo rat is a specialist that can chisel off the salty outer layer of certain plant leaves to feed on the much more succulent inner part. The Chisel-toothed Kangaroo rat is found around 5,600 years ago and onward. This suggests that perhaps there was a change in environment, going from a range of brushy shrubs to abundant saltbrush and shadscale.

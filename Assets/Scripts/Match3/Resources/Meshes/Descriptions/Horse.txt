Horses lived in North America long before European contact, but went extinct on the continent around 10,000 years ago. The bones of a horse closely related to the modern horse are found in the Paisley Caves archeological site from around 14500 to 13200 years ago. They're rare at the Paisley Caves but very common at Fossil Lake where they are found from more than 646,000 years ago to around 14,000 years ago. They are especially common around 100,000 years ago, and scientists think that at this time something caused many horses to come to the lakeside to drink, only to get trapped in particularly sticky mud.

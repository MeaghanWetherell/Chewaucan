The Tui Chub is a common fish in the lakes and streams of the deserts of western North America. It is well adapted to live in warm and salty waters, though some of that is behavioral. When the waters get too salty, the fish start to move towards the shores and inlets where the salt is less extreme. Tui chub bones are found in the Paisley Caves and at Fossil Lake, showing they've been present in Oregon for hundreds of thousands of years.
